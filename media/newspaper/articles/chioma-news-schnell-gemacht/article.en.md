# Done in seconds – but is it still yours?

Imagine: you need an image for your school poster. Before: draw it yourself, cut it out, glue it. Today: type three words — done. In two seconds.

The result looks better than anything you could have managed in an hour.

But something about it feels a bit odd. Right?

## What AI can do now

AI can today in seconds:
- Write texts (essays, summaries, poems)
- Create images (photorealistic, cartoon, watercolour — anything)
- Compose music
- Generate videos

And that's not getting harder. It's getting **easier**.

## The real question

The question is no longer: **"Can I do that with AI?"**

The question is: **"What's left of me then?"**

If AI makes the image, who made the image?
If AI writes the text, whose thoughts are those?

[[callout kind=reflect title=Think about it]]
A tool extends your hand. A paintbrush doesn't paint by itself. But what if the tool starts supplying the ideas too?
[[/callout]]

## Two real situations

**Situation 1: The poster**

Yasmin types "climate change, colourful, student" — AI spits out an image. She prints it, sticks it on. Done.

The image looks good. But did she learn anything about climate change? Did she think about what she wanted to show?

**Situation 2: The essay**

Lukas writes first by himself — a rough version, but his thoughts. Then he asks AI: "What could I improve?" AI gives suggestions. Lukas decides what to use.

The result is better. **And it's still his.**

## What matters

I think the difference isn't in the result — it's in the journey.

- Did you decide yourself what you wanted to say?
- Did you use AI — or did AI use you?
- Could you explain why the result is the way it is?

**If you can answer yes: then it's still yours.**

[[callout kind=tip title=Chioma's thought]]
AI can be an incredible tool. But the best thing you can bring is something AI doesn't have: your real opinion, your story, your perspective.
[[/callout]]

## What I hope for

Not less technology. But more awareness of what we're actually doing — and why.

The ability to create something that truly comes from you: that's going to become more valuable in the future, not less.

## And you?

When do you use AI — and when do you want to do it yourself? Are there things where you consciously say: I'm doing this without help?

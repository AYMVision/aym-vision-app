# 🤖 Aylin's AI Guide: Stay safe

# 🤖 Using AI

Many AI programmes have age rules.  
👉 And that's a good thing. They're there to protect you.

Only use AI with your parents' permission.  
Best of all: get started together with an adult.

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden1" alt="Your brain needs offline time too."]]

## ⭐ The three golden AI rules

[[checklist title=""]]
- I protect myself (no real data, no secrets)
- I check what I receive (AI can be wrong)
- I'm fair to others (label AI content, don't deceive anyone)
[[/checklist]]

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden2" alt="Your brain needs offline time too."]]

## 💬 How to talk to AI

You write to an AI like in a chat.

Here's what works well:
- Write politely
- Say clearly what you want
- Ask again if you don't understand something

[[callout kind="tip" title="Example"]]
"Please explain how a volcano forms. Explained simply for children."
[[/callout]]

[[callout kind="info" title="Important"]]
AI is not a person. Even if it writes like one.
[[/callout]]

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden3" alt="Your brain needs offline time too."]]

## 🔒 What you can enter — and what you must never enter

[[checklist title="This is okay"]]
- made-up names
- general questions
- ideas and stories
- help with learning
[[/checklist]]

[[callout kind="warn" title="Warning"]]
Never enter:
- your real name  
- address, school or class  
- phone number or passwords  
- photos of yourself or others  
- secrets  
[[/callout]]

👉 Remember:  
Anything you wouldn't tell a stranger doesn't belong in AI either.

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden4" alt="Your brain needs offline time too."]]


## 🧠 You decide

AI asks the questions.  
But you decide whether you answer.

You may:
- skip questions
- write "I don't want to say that"
- end the chat at any time

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden5" alt="Created with the help of AI."]]


## 🤔 Are AI answers always right?

No.

- AI can be wrong  
- AI sometimes makes things up  
- AI often sounds confident, even when it's wrong  

[[callout kind="tip" title="Tip"]]
Always check important information somewhere else too.  
You can also ask:  
"Give me trustworthy sources for this." and then you can check them.
[[/callout]]



## 📚 AI for school

AI may help you:
- explain things ✔️  
- support you while learning ✔️  
- gather ideas ✔️  

AI may not:
- do your homework for you ❌  
- simply write texts for you ❌  

👉 If AI helped you, write:  
"Created with the help of AI."



## ⚖️ AI images & texts: stay honest

AI can create images and texts that look very real.

This can:
- frighten others  
- confuse people  
- or make someone sad  

👉 That's why:  
If you create something with AI and share it, always say so.

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-aylin-kileitfaden/kileitfaden6" alt="Created with the help of AI."]]

## ⏸️ How much AI is okay?

AI is exciting but not an all-day thing.

- Take regular breaks  
- Don't use it constantly
- Follow your parents' rules  

👉 Your brain needs offline time too.

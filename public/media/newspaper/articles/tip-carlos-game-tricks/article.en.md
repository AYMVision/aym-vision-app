# Carlos checks: The tricks of online games

[[img src="media/newspaper/articles/tip-carlos-game-tricks/game-tricks1" alt="Carlos sitting on the sofa with his phone."]]

I just wanted a quick look.

Really.

Just a quick look.

Then suddenly way more time had passed than planned. And that's when I thought: wait a minute, amigo … why does this happen so often? 🎮

Lots of online games are pretty cleverly built. They use tricks that our brains know well: reward, anticipation, surprise, and the feeling of not wanting to miss out.

[[callout kind="info" title="Staying in control"]]
These tricks aren't automatically bad. They can also help: with learning, with sport, or when building a good habit. It becomes a problem when you're no longer really deciding for yourself, but just reacting. Like I did just now.
[[/callout]]

## Trick 1: The daily gift 🎁

Many games give you something every day: coins, XP, a crate or a small extra.

> I'll just quickly collect my reward.

[[details title="Why is this so appealing?"]]
Because small rewards quickly become a habit. Your brain notices: when I come back, I get something. And boom — you open the game even though you didn't really want to play.
[[/details]]

## Trick 2: The streak trap 🔥

Some games count how many days in a row you've shown up.
Day 1. Day 2. Day 3. Day 27.
And suddenly missing a day feels like losing.

> I can't break my streak.

[[details title="Carlos explains"]]
A streak feels like something you already own. That's why you don't want to lose it. Even when the reward isn't actually that important.
[[/details]]

## Trick 3: Today only! ⏰

Event ends at 11:59 pm.

Skin available this weekend only.

Special mission only now.

The game is basically saying:

> Better come now, or you'll miss out.

[[details title="What happens in your head?"]]
Scarcity makes things more exciting. When something is about to disappear, it seems more valuable. This is also called FOMO: fear of missing out.
[[/details]]

## Trick 4: The mystery box 🎲

You open a crate.

Maybe something rare is inside.

Maybe not.

And that's exactly what makes it irresistible.

> Maybe this time!

[[callout kind="warn" title="Watch out"]]
Uncertain rewards can pull particularly hard. Your brain hopes for the big win every time.
[[/callout]]

## Trick 5: Everyone's online 👥

Many games show you who's currently playing, who's already further ahead, or who has new items.

Then it's no longer just about the game itself.

It's also about belonging.

> If everyone's playing, I'll pop in for a bit too.

[[details title="Why is this so powerful?"]]
People want to belong. When friends are online, the game suddenly feels more important. Even when you actually wanted to do something else.
[[/details]]

## Carlos' quick check 🧠

Recognise any of these thoughts?

[[checklist title="Ever experienced this?"]]
- I just wanted a quick look.
- I didn't want to break my streak.
- I played longer because of an event.
- I wanted to know what was in a crate.
- I went online because others were online too.
[[/checklist]]

If you nodded inwardly at any point: no drama.

Then you've spotted the trick.

And that's already the first step.

[[callout kind="tip" title="Carlos' tip"]]
Ask yourself while playing: Am I playing because I genuinely feel like it — or because the game is pulling me in?
[[/callout]]

Games are allowed to be fun. Claro.

But when you know the tricks, you can decide for yourself.

And that's the real power move. 🎮🧠

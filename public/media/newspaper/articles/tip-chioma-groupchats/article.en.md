# Chioma’s Guide to Group Chats 💬

So you don’t have to learn the hard way from your own mistakes.  
Or simply:  
**For everyone who doesn’t want stress, fake news, or trouble.**

---

## Group chats – how it works

### ❓ 1. Why rules?
Without rules, chats become stressful and unfair –  
and sooner or later, they get deleted.

With rules, chats are fun:
- laughing
- sharing information
- voting together

👉 **Everyone benefits.**

---

## The chat rules
👉 Rules only work if **everyone helps decide them**.

---

### 📌 A) What belongs in the chat – and what doesn’t
- ✔️ Stay on topic.  
  No spamming. Pictures only once.
- ✔️ No fake news.  
  “I heard that …” doesn’t count.
- ✔️ Private stays private.  
  Personal stuff doesn’t belong in group chats.
- ✔️ No photos without permission.  
  People decide for themselves.

---

### 🤝 B) How we treat each other
- ✔️ No attacks on people.  
  No insults, threats, or shaming.
- ✔️ Don’t heat things up.  
  No laughing emojis, no forwarding, no cheering on.

---

### 🤔 C) When things get tense
- ✔️ Think first, then send.  
  In conflict: take a short break.
- ✔️ “Stop” means stop.  
  Then things get clarified.

---

### 🛡️ D) Order & responsibility
- ✔️ More than one admin.  
  No one decides alone.
- ✔️ Wrong content can be deleted.  
  Fake news, insults, or private content get removed – no discussion.

---

## ⛔ What happens if rules are broken
👉 This must be clear to everyone.

### How we solve problems:
1. A warning in the chat  
2. short chat pause  
3. get help (parents / teacher)

> 🎯 **Goal:** solve the problem, not hurt people.
